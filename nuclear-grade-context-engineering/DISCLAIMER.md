## Appropriate use

Use Nuclear-grade to improve:

- how clearly you state what a change must do and why;
- discipline about keeping the approved version under control;
- the trace from each claim to its evidence;
- verification and release readiness;
- trust decisions about dependencies, models, and APIs;
- controls for AI-assisted development;
- learning from incidents and fixing the cause;
- matching the rigor to the stakes.

Do not use Nuclear-grade on its own to justify regulated operation, safety significance, procurement acceptance, licensing, certification, or the adequacy of a formal quality program.

Do not describe a Nuclear-grade packet as formal V&V, NQA-1 evidence, regulator-facing evidence, safety-basis evidence, procurement evidence, or a quality-assurance record unless an authorized organization has separately adopted, reviewed, and approved that use under its governing requirements.
