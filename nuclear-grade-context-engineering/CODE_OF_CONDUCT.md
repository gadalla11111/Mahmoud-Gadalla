# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, the members, contributors, and leaders, pledge to make taking part in our community free of harassment for everyone. This holds no matter your age, body size, visible or hidden disability, ethnicity, sex traits, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, income, nationality, looks, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that build an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Behavior that helps build a good community:

- Showing empathy and kindness toward others.
- Respecting different opinions, points of view, and experiences.
- Giving helpful feedback, and accepting it with grace.
- Owning our mistakes, apologizing to the people we affect, and learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best for the whole community.

Behavior we will not accept:

- Sexual language or images, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insults, put-downs, and personal or political attacks.
- Harassment in public or in private.
- Sharing someone's private information, such as a home or email address, without their clear permission.
- Other conduct that a reasonable person would see as out of place at work.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers must make these standards clear and enforce them. They will take fair, fitting action when they judge behavior to be out of place, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces. It also applies when a person officially represents the project in public.

## Enforcement

You can report abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior through GitHub's private vulnerability reporting if it is turned on. If it is not, open an issue that asks for a private way to make contact, and leave out the sensitive details.

We will review and look into every complaint quickly and fairly. Maintainers must protect the privacy and safety of anyone who reports an incident.
