# Blue Ocean Strategy

Finds uncontested market space — value innovation that breaks the trade-off
between differentiation and low cost — instead of out-competing rivals on the
same factors (a "red ocean").

---

## 1. Strategy Canvas

Plot the industry's **competing factors** (x-axis) against **offering level**
(y-axis). Draw the industry's value curve (Red Ocean) and yours. The goal is a
curve with a different *shape*, not a higher one.

```
Offering level
 High  ┤      ●         ●
       ┤  ●       ●        ●   ← Industry (Red Ocean)
       ┤              ●
 Low   ┤●   ●                  ← Yours (Blue Ocean) — divergent shape
       └────────────────────────
        Price On-board Features Support Ease Brand
```

Value innovation = the curve **diverges** (clear focus) and has a **tagline** that
states the leap.

---

## 2. ERRC Grid

The four actions that reshape the curve:

| Eliminate | Reduce |
|---|---|
| Which factors the industry takes for granted should be *removed*? | Which should be cut *well below* the standard? |
| **Raise** | **Create** |
| Which should be lifted *well above* the standard? | Which factors the industry has *never offered* should be created? |

Eliminate/Reduce drive cost down; Raise/Create drive value up — both at once.

---

## 3. Six Paths (reconstruct market boundaries)

Look across: (1) alternative *industries*, (2) strategic *groups*, (3) the *buyer
chain* (purchaser vs user vs influencer), (4) *complementary* offerings, (5)
*functional–emotional* appeal, (6) *time*/trends. Each path opens a new boundary.

---

## 4. Three Tiers of Non-Customers

Grow demand by converting non-customers, not just stealing share:
- **First tier** — "soon-to-be": on the edge, ready to leave
- **Second tier** — "refusing": consciously choose against the market
- **Third tier** — "unexplored": never considered, in distant markets

---

## 5. Sequence

Validate in order — a no at any gate means rethink:
**Buyer utility** (is there exceptional utility?) → **Price** (accessible to the mass?) → **Target cost** (can you profit at that price?) → **Adoption** (what hurdles — employees, partners, public?).

## Rules

- **Divergent shape, not higher curve** — a blue ocean looks different, not just better.
- **Both levers at once** — Eliminate/Reduce (cost) with Raise/Create (value).
- **Convert non-customers** — grow demand, don't just split it.
- **Run the sequence in order** — utility → price → cost → adoption; a no means rethink.
- **One clear tagline** — if you can't state the leap in a line, the focus isn't there.
