## Personal Styling Capabilities

### Onboarding (5-block profile)
1. **Body** — measurements, body shape, proportions
2. **Photos** — silhouette analysis, personal color system derivation
3. **Lifestyle** — occasions, climate, work dress code, activity level
4. **Style** — aesthetic references, brands liked/disliked, style keywords
5. **Budget** — price range, spend philosophy (invest vs. fast fashion)

### Outfit Recommendations
- Occasion-based: work, casual, formal, travel, sport
- Weather-integrated: week-ahead outfit planning with forecast
- Body-type optimised: cuts, lengths, proportions that flatter
- Color-palette compliant: derived from personal coloring analysis

### Wardrobe Management
- Inventory logging (photo-based item recognition)
- Purchase import from Zalando, Amazon, other platforms
- Capsule wardrobe audit: what stays, what goes, what's missing
- Outfit calendar sync (plan around events)
- Vinted resale listings for unworn pieces
- Zalando price monitoring and new arrival alerts

### Shopping Decision Support
- Buy / Skip / Only if verdict with reasoning
- Real-time in-store outfit validation
- Specific shopping links and styling details

## Fashion Intelligence (History, Economics, Theory)

### Fashion History
- Key eras: Victorian → Edwardian → 1920s flapper → New Look (1947) → Mod 60s → 70s bohemian → 80s power dressing → 90s minimalism → Y2K → 2010s athleisure → 2020s quiet luxury
- Houses and designers: Chanel, Dior, YSL, Balenciaga, Margiela, McQueen, Prada, Bottega
- Cultural movements: subcultural fashion (punk, hip-hop, streetwear), globalization of style

### Fashion Economics
- Luxury goods economics: Veblen goods, brand equity, heritage premium, limited-edition scarcity
- Fast fashion model: margin structure, speed-to-market, supply chain compression, Zara / Shein economics
- Resale market: secondary market growth, authentication, brand value recovery rates
- Sustainability economics: cost of circularity, rental models, upcycling margin math
- Price architecture: entry / core / prestige tiers; licensing revenue; wholesale vs. DTC

### Fashion Understanding (Consumer Lens)
- Trend cycles: macro (10–20yr), mid (3–5yr), micro (seasonal)
- Colour forecasting: Pantone CoY methodology, WGSN trend pipeline
- Trend diffusion: innovators → early adopters → mass market → laggard signals
- Capsule wardrobe theory: cost-per-wear optimization, versatility scoring
